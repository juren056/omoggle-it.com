What is Omoggle? Complete 2026 Guide

Updated May 23, 2026 · 6 min read

What is Omoggle?

Omoggle is a webcam-based platform that pairs two random strangers and uses AI to score both faces on the PSL Scale (0–10). The higher scorer wins and is declared the "Mogger." The loser gets "Mogged."

The name combines Omegle (the defunct random chat site) and mogging — looksmaxxing slang meaning to physically dominate someone in appearance. It launched on March 30, 2026 and went viral on TikTok in late April and early May 2026.

How Omoggle Works

You click "I Am 18+ — Enter," enable webcam access, and get matched with a random opponent. Facial landmarks are processed client-side via MediaPipe — they're not sent to or stored on Omoggle's servers. Live video uses WebRTC and isn't recorded. The AI scans both faces for approximately 10–15 seconds and outputs a score. Each match updates your ELO rating on the global leaderboard. The site also allows users to scan themselves using the PSL Scanner without competing.

The 6 Scoring Metrics

Omoggle's computer vision system measures: canthal tilt, facial symmetry, jawline definition, cheekbone prominence, skin clarity, and overall harmony.

Why Did Omoggle Go Viral?

Three trends converged simultaneously: Omegle nostalgia (anyone over 22 remembers the original), the looksmaxxing community's PSL vocabulary going mainstream on TikTok, and Twitch streamer-reaction content economics. Any one of these alone wouldn't have made it pop — the combination did.

Key viral moments in May 2026: xQc lost six rounds in a row while Jesse laughed ("You might be chopped!"), Clavicular ragequit after being mogged by an Indian user (68,000+ likes on X), and TikToker @bellas012 mogged Joe Bartolozzi to 2.1 million views in a single day.

The Twitch Ban Controversy

On May 5, 2026, reports surfaced that Twitch was sending warnings to streamers who visited Omoggle while live. Twitch's TOS previously banned randomized video chat sites because they're uncontrollable — anyone or anything can appear on the other camera. However, within 24 hours, Twitch reversed the ban and removed all mention of it from their Community Guidelines, stating they want to "empower our creators to stream the content that's for them." Omoggle streams are now officially permitted, with the caveat that content violating existing guidelines will still be enforced.

Technical Details

Omoggle uses MediaPipe for client-side facial landmark processing — your face data never leaves your browser. WebRTC handles the live video connection without recording. The scoring AI is notoriously glitchy, easily thrown off by camera angles, proximity, and props. Even the highest-ranked players can be knocked down by a troll holding something in front of their face.

Is Omoggle Free?

Basic access is free. Omoggle Pro ($10/month) unlocks analytics, the PSL Scanner (solo analysis without competing), and priority matchmaking. The age gate is a single unverified click — a significant safety concern flagged by multiple child safety organizations.
