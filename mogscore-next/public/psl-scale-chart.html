PSL Scale Chart: Every Score, Band & Metric Weight

Updated July 20, 2026

PSL is the 1–10 face-rating scale Omoggle and the wider looksmaxxing community use as shorthand for "how would a tough crowd grade this face?" This chart shows what each band actually means, how the underlying metrics are weighted, and why climbing the top of the scale is so much harder than the middle.

PSL Score Chart (1–10)

PSL Band Label What It Means
9.0–10.0 MOG Top ~1%. Elite, model-tier features. Extremely rare.
7.5–8.9 Chad / Stacy Conventionally very attractive; stands out in any room.
6.0–7.4 High-Tier Normie (HTN) Clearly above average. Good structure with minor weak points.
4.0–5.9 Mid-Tier Normie (MTN) Where most people land. Average, unremarkable in either direction.
1.0–3.9 Sub-rated Below average by the scale's harsh standards. Often fixable with the highest-ROI basics.

The 5 Metrics — and How They're Weighted

A PSL score isn't one number pulled from thin air. It's a weighted blend of five sub-scores, each normalized to 0–100 and then mapped onto the 1–10 band. The weights are not equal:

  • Facial harmony — highest weight. How well features fit together in proportion. Stable, hard to fake, so it counts most.
  • Symmetry — high weight. Balance between the left and right halves of the face.
  • Jawline & cheekbones — medium weight. Bone structure and definition. Real, but the single most lighting-sensitive reading on the panel.
  • Canthal tilt — lower weight. The angle of the outer eye corners. Influential in culture, but a smaller slice of the score.
  • Skin clarity — lowest weight. Deliberately down-weighted because skin can be distorted by one bad night's sleep or a dim room.

Harmony and symmetry carry more than skin and tilt precisely because they're the traits that don't swing wildly between sessions. (Weights are approximate and not publicly documented to the decimal.)

Why the Scale Is Non-Linear

The jump from PSL 5.0 to 6.0 is a small raw-score move. The jump from 7.0 to 8.0 is a much bigger one. That's by design: most faces cluster in the middle of the distribution, so the top bands are compressed and represent increasingly rare feature combinations. In practice, going from "average" to "above average" is achievable with basics; going from "attractive" to "top 1%" mostly isn't.

How PSL Maps to Omoggle Tiers

Omoggle wraps the PSL score in an ELO ladder. Roughly: sub-rated maps to Molecule/Sub3, mid-tier to LTN/MTN, high-tier normie to HTN, Chad/Stacy to Chadlite/Chad, and MOG toward Slayer. In practice the very top tiers (Chad 3,501+, Slayer 5,001+, Adam 20,001+) are nearly empty. For the full ladder, see the Omoggle Tier List 2026.

Remember: It's One Input, Not a Verdict

PSL is a pseudoscientific approximation. Two faces with identical PSL can read completely differently in motion, and on Omoggle the actual mog battle is audience-judged — smiles, eye contact and confidence routinely beat a higher static score. Treat the number as feedback, not a ceiling.
