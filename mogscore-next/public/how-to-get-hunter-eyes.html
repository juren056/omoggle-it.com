How to Get Hunter Eyes: 7 Methods Ranked by Evidence

Updated June 1, 2026 · 8 min read

Hunter eyes are the most searched facial feature in looksmaxxing in 2026. They score at approximately 18% weight on Omoggle's AI — making them the second most impactful metric after facial symmetry. This guide covers what hunter eyes actually are, the 7 methods ranked by evidence, and what you can realistically achieve without surgery.

What Are Hunter Eyes Exactly?

The Anatomy Behind the Look

Hunter eyes are defined by four characteristics that work together: positive canthal tilt (outer eye corner sits at or above inner corner), deep-set placement with the brow ridge forward, hooded upper eyelids with minimal lid exposure, and minimal scleral show (little white visible below the iris).

The opposite — round, wide-open eyes with visible white below the iris and a negative canthal tilt — is called "prey eyes" in looksmaxxing terminology. Hunter eyes score significantly higher on Omoggle's canthal tilt metric. See: Canthal Tilt Guide.

Why Hunter Eyes Matter on Omoggle

18% of Your Total Score

Omoggle's computer vision system weights canthal tilt at approximately 18% of your total score. The eye area also contributes to overall harmony (12%), meaning the eye region influences roughly 25-30% of your final Omoggle score. This is why optimizing your camera angle — which emphasizes whatever positive canthal tilt you have — produces such consistent score improvements.

7 Methods to Get Hunter Eyes

Method 1 — Body Fat Reduction (Highest ROI)

Higher body fat creates periorbital fat deposits that make eyes appear rounder and more exposed. Getting lean to 12-15% body fat tightens the eye area and often reveals positive canthal tilt that was always there. This is the highest-ROI intervention for most people — it costs nothing and affects multiple metrics simultaneously. See: Face Fat Loss Guide.

Method 2 — Camera Angle Optimization (Immediate)

Positioning your camera 15-25cm above eye level makes you look slightly upward, naturally emphasizing positive canthal tilt in how the AI reads your facial landmarks. This is the fastest way to improve your eye area score on Omoggle — works in seconds, no physical change required. See: Camera Angle Guide.

Method 3 — Back Sleeping

Sleeping on your side compresses periorbital tissue asymmetrically, creating puffiness and apparent rounded eyes over time. Consistent back sleeping reduces this puffiness and prevents long-term asymmetric adaptation. Results appear in 2-3 weeks. See: Sleep Looksmaxxing.

Method 4 — Reduce Periorbital Puffiness

Alcohol, high sodium, poor sleep, and allergies all cause periorbital swelling that increases scleral show and worsens canthal tilt reading. Eliminating these shows results within 24-72 hours. Caffeine-based eye cream reduces morning puffiness short-term.

Method 5 — Mewing (Long Term)

Proper tongue posture may influence maxillary position over time, which affects the undereye area. Evidence in adults is limited but the habit is free and has no downside. See: Mewing Guide.

Method 6 — Styling

Eyebrow grooming that creates a slight arch toward the outer corner emphasizes positive canthal tilt visually. A haircut that clears the forehead and temples exposes the full eye area. These are immediate changes with meaningful visual impact.

Method 7 — Surgery (Permanent)

Lateral canthoplasty directly repositions the outer eye corner upward, creating permanent positive canthal tilt. This is a significant surgical procedure. It is the only intervention that directly addresses bone-level canthal tilt limitations — all other methods optimize around existing structure.
