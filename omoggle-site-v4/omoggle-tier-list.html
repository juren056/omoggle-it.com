Omoggle Tier List: All 8 Ranks Decoded

The Official Omoggle Tier List

Omoggle uses an ELO-based ranking system with eight distinct tiers. Your ELO changes with every match — wins raise it, losses lower it. The tier you land in is determined by your total ELO points, not your raw face score.

All 8 Tiers Explained

Molecule (0–500 ELO)

The starting tier. Most new players begin here. It's also the tier assigned when the AI scores a face very low — often due to poor lighting, an extreme angle, or a non-face image. Don't take a Molecule result seriously; fix your camera setup first.

Sub3 (501–1,000 ELO)

Still below average on the PSL Scale. Players in Sub3 have accumulated enough losses to sit near the bottom of the ranked pool. Consistent improvement in camera angle and skincare can help climb out of this tier.

LTN — Low Tier Normie (1,001–1,500 ELO)

Below-average facial features by PSL standards. Weak jawline, poor canthal tilt, or low symmetry are common factors. LTN is highly improvable with targeted looksmaxxing.

MTN — Mid Tier Normie (1,501–2,000 ELO)

The most common tier on the leaderboard. Average in every way — symmetric enough, decent jawline, average canthal tilt. This is the median Omoggle player.

HTN — High Tier Normie (2,001–3,000 ELO)

Clearly above average. Strong facial structure with a defined jawline, good cheekbones, and neutral-to-positive canthal tilt. Top 10–15% of the player base.

Chadlite (3,001–3,500 ELO)

Rare. This is the highest tier currently occupied on Omoggle's global leaderboard — the number one player sits at approximately 3,210 ELO. Chadlite requires exceptional facial geometry: strong positive canthal tilt, sharp jawline, high symmetry, and good cheekbone definition.

Chad (3,501–4,000 ELO)

Currently vacant. No player has reached this tier as of May 2026. Chad-tier requires near-perfect PSL scores across all six measured metrics simultaneously.

Slayer (4,001+ ELO)

Theoretical. Essentially unachievable. Represents a face that would score 9.0+ on the PSL Scale consistently — a level of facial perfection that may not exist outside CGI.
