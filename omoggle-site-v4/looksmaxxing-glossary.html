Looksmaxxing & Omoggle Glossary: Every Term Defined

The Complete Looksmaxxing Glossary

New to Omoggle or looksmaxxing? This glossary defines every term you'll encounter — from basic concepts to niche community slang. Bookmark this page for quick reference.

A–C

Blackpill

The belief that physical appearance is the primary determinant of life outcomes and is largely fixed by genetics. A philosophical position within looksmaxxing communities, ranging from acceptance to defeatism. Distinguished from "looksmaxxing" which is action-oriented.

Canthal tilt

The angle formed between the inner and outer corners of the eye. Positive canthal tilt (outer corner higher) is associated with attractiveness in both male and female faces. One of Omoggle's six scored metrics. Full explanation: canthal tilt guide.

Chad

A PSL tier representing 8.0–8.9 on the 10-point scale. Exceptional facial structure. Currently empty on Omoggle's global leaderboard as of May 2026.

Chadlite

PSL tier 7.0–7.9. The highest occupied tier on Omoggle's current leaderboard. Genuinely rare — requires strong positive canthal tilt, defined jawline, and high facial symmetry.

D–L

ELO

A competitive ranking system borrowed from chess. Omoggle uses ELO to rank players based on cumulative wins and losses against opponents of varying strength. Full explanation: ELO system guide.

Facial symmetry

How closely the left and right halves of a face mirror each other. Omoggle's highest-weighted single metric. Even minor symmetry improvements translate to score gains. See: facial symmetry guide.

Gymcel

A person who pursues gym training specifically for its looksmaxxing effects — to reduce facial fat, increase testosterone, and improve overall physical presentation. See: gym face guide.

HTN (High Tier Normie)

PSL tier 6.0–6.9. Clearly above-average facial features. Top 10–15% of the Omoggle player base.

Jawline

The mandible (lower jaw) boundary. Sharp, defined jawlines score higher on the PSL Scale. Improvable through fat loss and mewing. See: jawline guide.

L–M

Looksmaxxing

The systematic practice of maximizing physical appearance through lifestyle, skincare, fitness, and posture changes. The community and culture around Omoggle. Full guide: looksmaxxing 101.

LTN (Low Tier Normie)

PSL tier 4.0–4.9. Below average facial features by PSL measurement. Improvable with consistent looksmaxxing.

Mewing

The practice of resting the tongue flat against the roof of the mouth as a default posture, claimed to improve jaw structure over time. Popularized by Dr. John Mew. See: mewing guide.

Mogger / Mogged

The winner and loser of an Omoggle match respectively. "To mog" means to outclass someone in appearance. See: mogging explained.

Molecule

The lowest Omoggle tier (0–500 ELO). Often assigned to poor lighting, extreme angles, trolling, or non-face images rather than genuinely low PSL scores.

MTN (Mid Tier Normie)

PSL tier 5.0–5.9. The most common Omoggle tier. Average features in all measured metrics.

P–S

PSL Scale

Physical, Status, Looks — a 1–10 facial attractiveness rating system used in looksmaxxing communities and adapted by Omoggle's AI. Full guide: PSL scale explained.

Slayer

The highest Omoggle tier (4,001+ ELO, 9.0+ PSL). Theoretical — no confirmed Slayer-tier player exists as of May 2026.

Sub3

PSL tier below 3.0, represented by the Omoggle tier name "Sub3" (501–1,000 ELO). Indicates significant facial features that score poorly under AI measurement.

T–Z

Tier list

The hierarchy of eight Omoggle ELO tiers from Molecule to Slayer. Full breakdown: tier list guide.
