What is Omoggle? The Complete 2026 Guide

What is Omoggle?

Omoggle is a webcam-based platform that pairs two random strangers and uses AI to score both faces on the PSL Scale (0–10). The higher scorer wins and is declared the Mogger. The loser is Mogged.

The name combines Omegle (the defunct random chat site) and mogging — internet slang from looksmaxxing culture meaning to physically dominate someone in appearance.

How Does It Work?

You enter Omoggle, complete a camera check, and get matched with a random opponent. The AI scans both faces for ~10 seconds and outputs a score. Each match adjusts your ELO rating, which determines your global leaderboard position.

The six scoring metrics

Omoggle's AI measures: facial symmetry, canthal tilt, jawline definition, cheekbone prominence, skin clarity, and overall facial harmony. Each contributes to your 0–10 score.

Why Did It Go Viral?

Omoggle exploded in April 2026 when major Twitch streamers began live-streaming sessions. Clips of xQc losing repeatedly and Clavicular ragequitting spread across TikTok and X. On May 5th, Twitch officially allowed Omoggle streams.

Is Omoggle Free?

Basic access is free. Omoggle Pro ($10/month) unlocks analytics and priority matching. The solo PSL Scanner mode is also free.
