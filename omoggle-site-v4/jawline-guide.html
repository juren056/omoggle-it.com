How to Improve Your Jawline for a Higher Mog Score

Why Jawline Matters on Omoggle

Jawline definition is one of the six primary metrics measured by Omoggle's AI. A sharp, defined mandible line scores significantly higher than a soft or undefined jaw. It's also the metric most responsive to lifestyle changes — making it the highest-ROI target for looksmaxxing.

3 Proven Approaches

1. Reduce body fat percentage

The most impactful change you can make. Facial fat (particularly in the lower face and neck) is the primary cause of an undefined jawline in most people. Reducing overall body fat through a moderate caloric deficit (500 kcal/day below maintenance) and consistent cardio will visibly sharpen jaw definition within 8–16 weeks. Aim for 12–15% body fat for optimal facial definition.

2. Compound gym training

Heavy compound lifts (squats, deadlifts, rows, overhead press) elevate testosterone and growth hormone, which contributes to facial masculinization over time including jaw and brow development. Gym training also indirectly improves jaw definition by reducing facial fat through metabolic improvement.

3. Mewing

Maintaining correct tongue posture (tongue flat on roof of mouth) applies consistent upward and forward pressure on the palate and jaw. Long-term practitioners report improved jaw definition, though results vary significantly and take months to years.

Exercises That Actually Help

Chewing hard foods (raw vegetables, tough meats, gum) can strengthen the masseter muscle, giving a slightly squarer jaw appearance. Mewing, neck training, and jaw exercises don't dramatically reshape bone structure in adults but contribute to overall muscle tone around the jaw and neck.
