Omoggle Beginner Guide: Everything You Need to Start

Quick Summary

Omoggle pairs two strangers via webcam and uses AI to score both faces on the PSL Scale. Higher scorer wins. Both gain or lose ELO.

Step-by-Step for Beginners

Step 1: Optimize your setup first

Before your first match, spend 10 minutes on camera setup. Most beginners skip this and immediately regret it.

Step 2: Camera check

Omoggle calibrates the AI to your webcam. Take this seriously — poor calibration results in inaccurate scoring.

Step 3: Your first match

Click Enter Arena. Keep a neutral, relaxed expression. The AI scans for ~10 seconds.

Step 4: Understand your score

Your score is recalculated fresh each match. ELO accumulates across all matches and determines your tier.

5 Tips for Beginners

1. Don't play on default laptop settings

Built-in webcams almost always underperform. Raise your laptop or get an external webcam.

2. Play in the morning

Post-sleep faces score measurably higher. See: sleep guide.

3. Accept early losses

First 10 matches are calibration. Don't adjust setup mid-session.

4. Use PSL Scanner first

Get your baseline solo before competing live.

5. Practice with our free tool

Our AI Face Analyzer scores you without a live opponent.
