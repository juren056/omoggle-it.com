What Does "Mogging" Mean? The Complete Definition

The Definition of Mogging

To mog someone means to physically dominate or outclass them in terms of appearance, presence, or any measurable quality. The person who wins is the Mogger; the person who loses is the one who has been Mogged.

According to Merriam-Webster, "mog" is a humorous internet slang term meaning "to outclass," used especially to describe one person as appearing far more attractive than another.

Where Does Mogging Come From?

The term derives from the phrase "alpha male of group" — AMOG — which was shortened and evolved into the verb "to mog." It originated in bodybuilding and fitness communities where it described one person physically dominating a space or social situation simply by their presence or physique.

Mogging in looksmaxxing culture

The term was adopted by looksmaxxing communities to describe facial dominance specifically. A person with a stronger jawline, better canthal tilt, or higher PSL score "mogs" someone with weaker features. Looksmaxxers began rating and comparing each other using structured scales, which eventually led to platforms like Omoggle.

Mogging on Omoggle

On Omoggle, mogging is formalized: two strangers face each other on webcam, the AI scans both, and declares one the Mogger. The "MOGGED" title appearing on the loser's side of the screen became a viral meme after clips from Twitch streams spread across social media.

Types of Mogging

In looksmaxxing culture, mogging can refer to different kinds of dominance. Height mogging means being significantly taller. Frame mogging refers to having a broader, more impressive body structure. Looksmogging or face mogging is the most commonly discussed — being judged as having superior facial features, which is what Omoggle measures directly.
